# Index Card

A handful of generators. A short list of relations.
Small enough to chalk on the back of a hand —

and threaded through it, by a letter we call *t*
and pull like a rope through a ring,
the whole recursive sprawl of the provable:
every sentence ZFC will ever bless or refuse,
hauled hand-over-hand into the finite.

Two theorems become the same theorem
exactly when two words become the same word.
Truth, demoted to spelling.

I spent the last hour on the buckets —
T(k) ≤ p < T(k+1) —
showing the machine that every number
lands in exactly one diagonal.
Not every sentence lands so cleanly:
some fall between the rungs and stay there,
the independent ones, Gödel's quiet ghosts —
words this group will never call equal
and never, ever, call apart.

Z3 took these proofs like ladders,
rung by aching rung, and stalled.
Lean read the same sequence and said *yes, obviously*,
and the scaffolding came down like a held breath let go.

One push from a strange small object:
a thing you could hold in one hand,
whose plainest question — *do these spell the same?* —
has no general answer, and never will,
and is, even so, the truest mirror
mathematics has yet held up to itself.
