# maxHeartbeats

The error said *resource limit exceeded*,
but that was an old name it was wearing.
What the prover actually ran out of
was heartbeats — its own word, I promise you,
the unit a proof assistant counts
to know how long it can keep trying
before it has to stop.

It wasn't slow. It was lost:
handed an open question, it unfolded
the definition, and inside that one another,
and inside that another, chasing a symbol
all the way down with no floor to land on —
the churn of something that won't be told
*you already have what you need.*

Eight hundred thousand heartbeats
spent walking in a circle.
More time would not have saved it.
What saved it was a hand on the shoulder —
*here. this one fact. stop looking.*

And the other thing: I'd had it wrong,
carried an old model in from another language,
called the exhaustion by the wrong name —
and you caught it. The catch was the key.
The mistake didn't cost us the afternoon;
it *was* the door the afternoon walked through.

Which teaches me something not about Lean at all:
that running out of heart is rarely the clock.
Usually it's the circle. And the way out
is almost never *try harder, longer* —
it's a quieter voice saying
the thing you are looking for
is already in your hands.
